Where it started

A family business, a brother
who never stopped working.

Shivendra's background

Shivendra grew up watching his family run a small home-service business. His brother was the one who built it from scratch — skilled at the trade, trusted by customers, and completely consumed by the work. Not just the field work. The phone calls. The scheduling. The follow-ups. The back-and-forth with customers who needed quotes. The admin that never seemed to end.

His brother was good at the business. He just never had time to grow it. Weekends that were supposed to be off turned into catch-up time for voicemails. Family dinners were interrupted by calls he couldn't afford to miss. Every missed call was a calculation — answer and stop what you're doing, or let it go and hope they call back.

"He wasn't failing at running a business. He was succeeding at it — while doing every job that should have had a person behind it."

Shivendra carried that observation into his career. He went on to work directly with multiple home-service businesses, helping them grow. And the same pattern appeared everywhere: contractors spending real money on Google Ads, referral networks, and Angi listings — then losing a substantial portion of those leads to voicemail, slow callbacks, and after-hours silence.

The money was going out. The calls weren't all coming back.

What the numbers revealed

Working across multiple businesses gave Shivendra a view that individual operators rarely get: the comparison. One HVAC company would convert 40% of its inbound calls into booked estimates. Another, nearly identical in service quality and market, converted barely 20%. The difference wasn't the technicians. It wasn't the pricing. It was response time and coverage.

The businesses that answered every call — or followed up within minutes — consistently won more jobs from the same marketing spend. The ones that didn't answer were quietly losing revenue they had already paid to generate.

The fix seemed obvious: hire someone to answer calls. But for a two-person shop or a five-person crew, a full-time receptionist is a significant fixed cost for variable lead flow. A generic answering service takes messages but doesn't understand what a broken heat exchanger means or whether a leaking pipe is an emergency. The gap wasn't staffing. It was intelligence.

That's the gap Wrenlo was designed to close.

The shared insight

Two different vantage points.
The same conclusion.

Shivendra had seen the revenue leak from the outside — watching contractors pay for leads that never converted because nobody was available to answer. Ashwini had seen the tooling gap from the inside — watching small businesses adopt software built for companies ten times their size, then blame themselves when it didn't fit.

When they compared notes, the picture was clear. Home-service contractors didn't need a new field-service management platform. Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, and Workiz already handle scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, and CRM well. The gap wasn't operations. The gap was the first 10 minutes of every customer interaction.

"The call is already ringing. The lead is already there. The only question is whether someone — or something — answers it."

Every contractor we spoke to during our research described the same problem in slightly different words. The plumber who missed a $3,000 burst-pipe call because he was under a sink. The HVAC owner who lost a replacement estimate to a competitor who answered 8 minutes faster. The electrician who found out — three months later — that a customer had called for a panel upgrade and never left a voicemail.

Ashwini's insight was on the tooling side. The solutions that existed for this problem were either too generic or too expensive. Generic AI receptionists could answer a call and take a message — but they didn't know the difference between a no-heat emergency and a routine maintenance request. Human answering services cost $300–$600 a month and produced the same unstructured message pad output.

Neither option booked anything. Neither option understood the trade. Neither option told the contractor how much revenue it recovered.

Wrenlo was designed to do all three. Answer calls with trade-specific intelligence. Book the right next step into the contractor's existing calendar or FSM. Show the owner exactly how much revenue was protected — in a dashboard that speaks the language of jobs, not call minutes.

That's the product we wish had existed for every contractor we've worked with. That's why we built it.

How we operate

The principles behind
every product decision.

🎯
Outcomes over features
Contractors don't want AI. They want fewer missed calls, more booked estimates, and less owner interruption. Every feature we build is measured against those outcomes — not against a feature checklist.
🔌
Works with your tools — not instead of them
Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, and Google Calendar are already running your operations. Wrenlo integrates with them as the front-office layer. We don't ask you to replace what's working.
🔍
Visible and auditable by default
Every call gets a transcript. Every automated action gets an audit trail. Owners can see exactly what Wrenlo said, what it booked, and what it flagged. No black boxes.
🏗️
Built narrow and deep — not wide and thin
We build specifically for residential home-service contractors — not every SMB, not every trade, not enterprise facilities management. Depth of fit beats breadth of coverage.
💰
Bootstrapped means we win when you win
No venture funding means no growth-at-all-costs pressure. We are profitable only when our customers stay and expand. That aligns our incentives directly with your results.
📞
Safety and trust are non-negotiable
Emergency scripts escalate — they don't troubleshoot. Booking rules require owner approval. Consent and opt-out handling are built in from day one, not added later as compliance afterthoughts.
